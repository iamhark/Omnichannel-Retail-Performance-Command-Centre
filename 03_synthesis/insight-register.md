| INS-01 | Champions are 16.3% of identified customers but generate 46.1% of monetary value; At Risk and Lost customers account for 30.5% of the base. The unsimulated UCI core has a 65.6% repeat-customer rate. | CLM-002, CLM-003, CLM-007 | Value is concentrated, while a separate large group shows recency/frequency deterioration. The combined-model 100% repeat rate is a deterministic simulated-store artifact, so the public-core rate is the valid portfolio KPI. | Retention resources should be tiered by segment and measured against a provenance-clean repeat-rate baseline. | RFM uses historical behavior, quintile thresholds, and simulated store activity; it does not measure future churn probability. | Protect Champions with service and loyalty treatment; run a capped reactivation test for At Risk customers; suppress Lost customers after two failed contacts. | Finding/action 1 | Customer page |
| INS-02 | Simulated Manchester is £94,542 (+18.0%) above its same-format peer baseline and Birmingham is £94,542 (-18.0%) below; Leeds is £44,976 (+11.4%) above and Bristol £27,233 (-6.9%) below. | CLM-004 | The paired format gaps create a controlled operational comparison: the most useful next step is to compare assortment, conversion, staffing, and returns between matched stores. | Management should not set uniform store targets until same-format operating differences are diagnosed. | Store identities, sales, and costs are simulated; the pattern tests the analytical workflow rather than estimates real locations. | Use Manchester-to-Birmingham and Leeds-to-Bristol as diagnostic pairs; transfer only practices that survive category-mix and return-rate checks. | Finding/action 2 | Product/store page |
| INS-03 | Simulated Social ROI is -47.0% with £421 CAC, while Email and Paid Search exceed +51% ROI with CAC near £165-£168. | CLM-005 | Social consumes similar spend but creates less attributed gross margin. The gap is large enough to justify a controlled budget test under the simulation assumptions. | Budget should move toward Email and Paid Search unless a holdout test shows unmeasured Social-assisted effects. | Costs, sessions, spend, and attribution are simulated; last-touch ROI does not estimate causal incrementality. | Cut Social prospecting allocation in the next simulated planning cycle, reallocate to Email and Paid Search, and reserve a holdout budget for incrementality testing. | Finding/action 3 | Marketing page |
